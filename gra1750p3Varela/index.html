Salmon Teriyaki

4 Servings.

Stuck in a salmon rut? We got you. You'll never believe how easy it is to
make your own sweet-salty teriyaki sauce at home, and there's no better
application for it than this beyond-simple weeknight favorite.


Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup sake
  • 1/4 cup mirin (sweet Japanese rice wine)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil, plus more if needed
  • 4-6-ounce-skin-on, boneless salmon fillets (about 1 inch thick)
  • Kosher salt

Steps

  1. Combine 1/2 cupsake, 1/4 cup mirin, and 1/4 cup soy sauce in a small bowl; set teriyaki sauce aside.
  2. Heat 1 Tbsp. oil in a large skillet over medium-high. Season 4 salmon fillets lightly with salt.
  3. Working in batches and adding more oil if needed, cook, skin side down, until skin is brown and crisp, about 4 minutes. Turn and cook until other side is just beginning to brown, about 2 minutes. Transfer to a plate.
  4. Pour off fat in skillet. Bring teriyaki sauce to a boil in skillet over medium heat. Cook until reduced by two-thirds, about 4 minutes.
  5. Add salmon, skin side up, and cook, spooning sauce over, until sauce is syrupy and salmon is just cooked through, about 2 minutes.
  6. Transfer to platter and serve with white rice, noodles, or a simple salad.
