Sweetness for the Soul

Strawberry Mousse Cake

For lovers of sweets, the Strawberry Mousse Cake is the sweetness you need to try. With the smoothness and fruitiness of the strawberry mousse, its appealing taste is divine. This no-bake cake can be made in the comfort of your home for yourself or to celebrate the holidays, Valentine’s Day, Mother’s Day, and other special occasions.

Ingredients

Cookie Base

  • 1½ cups (240 g) of Golden Oreo, which is about 24 cookies
  • 4 tablespoons (59 g) of unsalted, melted butter

Strawberry Mousse

  • 4 teaspoons of unflavored gelatin powder
  • 3 tablespoons of cold water
  • 1¾ cups (420 ml) heavy whipped cream
  • 4 ounces (113 g) of Mascarpone cheese, or cream cheese
  • 1 cup (120 g) of confectioners’ sugar, sifted
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups (425 g) fresh chopped strawberries
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice

Toppings

  • 1½ cups (230 g) fresh chopped strawberries
  • ¼ cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of unflavored gelatin powder
  • 2 tablespoons of cold water
  • whipped cream, which is optional

Steps

Making the Base

  1. Line the inside base of the 9-inch springform pan with aluminum foil or parchment paper. Set it aside for now.
  2. Place the Oreos in a food processor until it is ground to fine crumbs.
  3. Melt the butter and stir with the crumbed Oreo until it is well coated.
  4. Press the crumbs tightly into the bottom of the pan. Make sure to apply an even base, and then set to freeze while you prepare the filling.

Making the Mousse

  1. Add the gelatin over the 3 teaspoons of cold water in a small microwave safe bowl and set aside.
  2. In a medium size bowl, combine the heavy cream, the mascarpone cheese, confectioners’ sugar, vanilla, and salt. Beat on medium low speed until whipped and fluffy; give it about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the strawberries and lemon juice to the bowl of a food processor and process until smooth. Run the puree through a fine mesh sieve and discard the seeds.
  4. Add ¼ of a cup of the strawberry puree into the gelatin. Heat in the microwave for 1 minute then stir until the gelatin is fully dissolved. Pour it back into the bowl with the remaining puree and stir to combine.
  5. Pour the strawberry mixture into the bowl with the whipped cream mixture. Use a silicone spatula to carefully fold everything together until well combined.
  6. Pour the strawberry mousse over the prepared crust. Use an offset spatula to smooth the top.
  7. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 2 hours.

The Strawberry Jam

  1. Sprinkle the gelatin over 2 tablespoons of cold water in a small microwave safe bowl and set aside.
  2. Puree the strawberries and sugar in a food processor until smooth. Run the puree through a fine mesh sieve and discard the seeds.
  3. Add ¼ cup of the puree to the gelatin and heat in the microwave for 1 minute. Stir until the gelatin is fully dissolved. Pour back into the bowl with the remaining puree and stir to combine.
  4. Pour the puree over the top of the mousse layer and swirl the pan slightly until the puree evenly covers the top.
  5. Cover the pan with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 6 hours or overnight. Once ready, add some whipped cream and enjoy your fruity cake.
