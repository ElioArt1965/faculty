Crêpes Suzette

By Raquel Quintanilla | Updated on September 17, 2023


Crêpes Suzette is a traditional French dessert of a very thin delicate pancake served with an orange and liqueur buttery sauce. The origin of the dish and its name is disputed. Henri Charpentier claimed to have accidentally invented it in 1895 when he was a 14-year-old assistant waiter at the Maitre at Monte Carlo’s Café de Paris. This happened during a reception for the Prince of Wales, whose guest was a girl named Suzette. A less fantastical version emerges during an interview in the 1950s where he explained that the dish was his interpretation of his foster mother's pancakes with fruit sauce. The original version did not include the flambée.

Here is my favorite recipe for this classic french dessert ideal for any weekend gathering with friends or family. I promise you will leave everyone asking for more!

10 mins 20 mins 30 mins 60 mins
COURSE CUISINE SERVING CALORIES
Anytime French 16 (8 people) 158

Ingredients

Crêpes

  • 3 large eggs
  • 200 gr all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 Tbsp granulated sugar
  • ⅛ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 50 gr unsalted butter melted
  • 1 teaspoon of Cuban rum
  • 1 Tbsp vegetable oil (canola or avocado oil)

Suzette Sauce

  • 100 gr unsalted softened butter
  • 80 gr of granulated sugar
  • 1 Tbsp finely grated mandarin zest
  • 2 Tbsp orange zest and rind for garnish
  • 2 mandarin juice
  • 50 gr unsalted butter melted
  • 2 Tbsp triple sec orange liqueur (Cointreau, Curaçao, Grand Marnier)


TIPS

🐝 Batter can be stored in an air-tight container in the fridge up to 2 days.

🐝 Crêpes can be made ahead 1-2 days ahead and store inside a silicone bag in the fridge.

🐝 Sugar can be substituted with monk fruit sweetener.

Intructions

Making the Crêpes

  1. Mix all the dry ingredients in a medium size bowl. Add the eggs one by one and whisk together. Pour the milk and whisk well.
  2. Add the butter and the rum to the mix making sure the texture is smooth. Reserve the batter in the fridge for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Heat a 10-inch crêpe skillet over medium-high and brush with vegetable oil. Add approximately 3 tbsp of the batter and quickly tilt skillet to evenly distribute it.
  4. When the edges of the crêpe curl up a bit and start to brown use a spatula to gently unstick the edges. Flip the crêpe and cook for 30 seconds on the other side.
  5. Transfer the crêpe to a platter. Repeat the process with the remaining batter. Wipe the skillet clean and lightly brush oil a few times as necessary in between crêpes.

Prepare the Suzette Sauce

Use a fork to mix the softened butter, sugar, and mandarin zest. Add the mandarin juice and the orange liqueur. The liquids will not totally mix up with the butter which is fine. Reserve at room temperature.

Wrapping the Crêpes Suzettes

  1. Transfer a crêpe back to the skillet over low heat. Use a spoon to spread the Suzette sauce mixture over the upper side of the crêpe evenly. Wait until it melts completely (30 or 40 seconds).
  2. Working with one crêpe at a time, fold the crêpe into fourths using a fork and a spoon. Lift and let excess sauce drip off.
  3. Transfer the folded crêpes to a platter (2 per person); spoon any extra sauce over top and sprinkle with orange zest/rind for decoration.
  4. Serve immediately.
