Sweet Esquites

Mexican Street Corn (Esquites)

Esquites, a delightful Mexican street food, transforms corn kernels into a flavor-packed treat. Served in a cup, the dish includes boiled or roasted corn coated in a mix of mayonnaise, lime juice, chili powder, and cotija cheese, creating a mouthwatering blend of creamy, tangy, and spicy flavors. Garnished with cilantro or green onions, esquites offer a convenient and satisfying snack, highlighting the vibrant taste of Mexican cuisine in handheld form.

Ingredients

  • Corn kernels (boiled or roasted)
  • Mayonnaise
  • Lime juice
  • Chili powder (such as Tajín or ancho chili powder)
  • Cotija cheese (or a similar crumbly cheese)
  • Optional garnishes: Chopped cilantro, Chopped green onions, or Hot sauce

Steps

  1. If using fresh corn, remove the kernels from the cob after boiling or roasting. If using frozen or canned corn, ensure it's cooked and drained.
  2. In a large bowl, add the boiled or roasted corn kernels. Mix in mayonnaise until the corn is evenly coated. The amount of mayonnaise can vary based on personal preference.
  3. Squeeze fresh lime juice over the corn and mayonnaise mixture. Adjust the amount to achieve the desired level of tanginess.
  4. Generously sprinkle chili powder over the corn. This adds a flavorful kick, so adjust according to your spice preference.
  5. Crumble cotija cheese (or a similar crumbly cheese) into the bowl. Mix well to distribute the cheese evenly throughout the esquites.
  6. If desired, add optional garnishes such as chopped cilantro and green onions. These add freshness and additional layers of flavor.
  7. Spoon the esquites into cups or bowls. Optionally, provide hot sauce on the side for those who enjoy an extra kick. Serve immediately while still warm.
  8. Feel free to customize the recipe based on your preferences. Some variations include adding butter, sour cream, or additional spices like cayenne pepper.
